# Description (EN)

**Helical Temporal Compression (HTC)** is a physical theory stating that *time is not a straight line*, but instead a **helix** (a helicoid) with a variable pitch. The pitch of the spiral represents the "density of time": the smaller the pitch, the slower time flows; the larger the pitch, the faster it flows.

This helical structure of time may *compress* or *expand* depending on matter, energy, and spatial conditions:

* **Dense matter** slows down time by *compressing the time spiral*.
* **Low-density environments** or vacuum barely affect the spiral ‚Äî time flows faster.
* **Pure energy** tends to straighten the spiral ‚Äî time becomes almost linear (the limit phase).

You can think of this as a **wave of time** propagating through space:

* In dense regions the wave slows down, the spiral pitch decreases.
* In empty space the wave accelerates, the pitch increases.

Just as light changes speed depending on optical density, **time adjusts its ‚Äúdensity‚Äù depending on the density of matter and energy**. This is the central idea of HTC: *time is a physical process, not merely a parameter*.

In HTC, the spiral pitch is expressed as **h(œÑ)**. It can be interpreted as a local propagation speed of temporal flow or a *temporal density indicator*. Near massive objects h(œÑ) decreases, slowing time. In empty space h(œÑ) increases, approaching its maximal value ‚Äî straight, uncompressed time.
